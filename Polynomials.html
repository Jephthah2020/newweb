POLYNOMIALS


Polynomial Functions




A polynomial function is a function such asa quadratic, a cubic, a quartic, involving only positive integer powers of a variable \( x \).

The degree of a polynomial is the highest power of \( x \) in its expression.

A polynomial of degree \( n \) is a function of the form

\( \displaystyle{p(x) = a_nx^n+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+ \cdots +a_2x^2+a_1x+a_0} \)

where \( \displaystyle{a_n, a_{n-1}, \cdots a_0} \) are real numbers called the coefficient of the polynomial.

For example

\( \displaystyle{p(x)= 3x^5-x^2+7} \)

here, the degree of the polynomial is \( 5 \).

NOTE: \( \displaystyle{x^{1 \over 2}+3} \) and \( \displaystyle{2x^7+ \frac{5}{x^3}} \) are not polynomials.

For a functionto be a polynomial function,the power of \( x \) must be "positive integers".

A polynomial of degree \( 0 \) is called a constant; \( \displaystyle{p(x)=2} \)

A polynomial of degree \( 1 \) is called a linear function; \( \displaystyle{p(x)= 5x+4} \)

A polynomial of degree \( 2 \) is called a quadratic function; \( \displaystyle{p(x) = 2x^2+x+3} \)

Algebra of Polynomials



The following operations can be carried out on polynomials :

(1) Addition : We can add two polynomials by adding the coefficients of terms having the same power.

Foe example, if

\( \displaystyle{f(x) =x^3-3x+4} \) and \( \displaystyle{2x^3+x-2} \), then

\( \displaystyle{f(x)-g(x) = (x^3-3x+4)-(2x^3+x-2)} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies x^3-3x+4-2x^3-x+2} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies -x^3-4x+6} \)


(2) Subtraction : We can subtract a polynomial from another.

For example, if

\( \displaystyle{ f(x) = x^3-3x+4} \) and \( \displaystyle{g(x) = 2x^3+x-2 } \) , then

\( \displaystyle{f(x) - g(x) = (x^3-3x+4)-(2x^3+x-2) } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies x^3-3x+4-2x^3-x+2 } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies -x^3-4x+6 } \)


(3)Multiplication : We can multiply two polynomials together.

For example,if

\( \displaystyle{ f(x) = 2x-1 } \) and \( \displaystyle{ g(x) = 3x^2+4x+2 } \) , then

\( \displaystyle{f(x) \cdot g(x) = (2x-1)(3x^2+4x+2) } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies 6x^3+8x^2+4x-3x^2-4x-2 } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies 6x^3+5x^2-2 } \)


(4) Division : We can divide a polynomial of a higher degree by a polynomial of a lesser degree.

This is similar to long division for ordinary numbers.

For example, let us divide

\( \displaystyle{x^3+5x^2+4x-17 } \) by \( \displaystyle{ x-4 } \)

here, we have two polynomials

\( \displaystyle{ f(x) = x^3+5x^2+4x-17 } \)

\( \displaystyle{ g(x) = x-4 } \)
