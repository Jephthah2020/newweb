INDICES & LOGARITHMS

INDICES

Laws of Indices

* \( a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n} \)
* \( a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n} \)
* \( \sqrt{a} = a^{1 \over 2} \)
* \(a^{-1} = {1 \over a} \)
* \( (a^m)^n = a^{mn} \)
* \(a^0 = 1 \)
* \(a^{m \over n} = (a^{1 \over n})^m = (\sqrt[m]{a})^m \)


Examples

(1)Express with positive indices : \( \sqrt[3]{abc^{-4}} \over \sqrt[4]{a^3b^{-3}c} \)
Solution
\( \sqrt[3]{(abc^{-4})} \over \sqrt[4]{(a^3b^{-3}c)} \)

\( = {(abc^{-4})^{1 \over 3} \over (a^3b^{-3}c)^{1 \over 4}} \)

\( = {a^{1 \over 3}b^{1\over 3}c^{-4 \over 3} \over a^{3 \over 4}b^{-3 \over 4}c^{1 \over 4}} \)

\( = {a^{1 \over 3} \over {a^{3 \over 4}}} \times {b^{ 1\over 3} \over {b^{-3 \over 4}}} \times {c^{-4 \over 3} \over {c^{1 \over 4}}} \)

\( = {a^{{1 \over 3} - {3 \over 4}}} \times {b^{{1 \over 3} - {-3 \over 4}}} \times {c^{{-4 \over 3} - {1 \over 4}}} \)

\( = {a^{-5 \over 12}}{b^{5 \over 12}}{c^{-19 \over12}} \)

\( = {1 \over {a^{5 \over 12}}} \)\({b^{5 \over 12}} \)\({1 \over {c^{19 \over 12}}}\)

\( = {{ b^{5 \over 12}} \over { {a^{5 \over 12}}{c^{19 \over 12}}}} \)


(2) Evaluate (a) \( {27}^{5 \over 3} \) (b) \( (36)^{-3 \over 2} \) (c) \( (16)^{-1 \over 4} \)

Solution

(a) \( {27}^{5 \over 3} = {(3^3)^{5 \over 3}} = {3^{3 \times {5 \over 3}}} = {3^5} = 243 \)


(b) \( {(36)^{-3 \over 2}} = {(6^2)^{-3 \over 2}} = {6^{2 \times {-3 \over 2}}} \)
\( = {6^{-3}} = \) \( {1 \over {6^3}} = {1 \over 216} \)


(c) \( (16)^{-1 \over 4} = {{(2^4)}^{-1 \over 4}} = {2^{-1}} = \)\({1 \over 2} \)


(3) Evaluate (a) \( (32)^{0.4} \) (b) \( (0.027)^{2 \over 3} \) (c) \( (2^{-3})^2 \)
Solution

(a) \( {(32)^{0.4} = {(32)^{4 \over 10}} = {(2^5)^{4 \over 10}} = {2^{5 \times {2 \over 5}}} = {2^2} = 4}\)


(b) \( (0.027)^{2 \over 3} \)\( = {\big({27 \over 1000}\big)^{2 \over 3}} = {\big({{3^3} \over {10}^3}\big)} = {\big[\big({3 \over 10}\big)^3\big]^{2 \over 3}} = {\big({3 \over 10}\big)^2} = {{3^2} \over {{10}^2}} = {9 \over 100} \)


(c) \( (2^{-3})^2 \) \( = {\big({1 \over {2^3}}\big)^2} = {\big({1 \over {2^{3 \times 2}}}\big)} = {1 \over {2^6}} = {1 \over 64} \)
