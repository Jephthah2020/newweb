COMPLEX NUMBERS



A number of the form
\( z = a+ib \)
where \( a \) and \( b \) are real numbers is called a complex number.

The set of complex numbers is denoted by \( \mathbb{C} \).

\( \mathbb{C} = \left\{ z = a+ib \ni a,b \in \mathbb{R} , i=\sqrt{-1} \right\} \)

Note :
\(\displaystyle{ i^2=-1} \)
\( \displaystyle{ i^3={-1(i)}= -i} \)
\( \displaystyle{i^4=i^2+i^2={-1 \cdot -1}=1} \)
\( \displaystyle{i^5=i \cdot i^4=i \cdot 1 = i} \)


Rules for the manipulation of complex numbers



(i) \( {a+ib} = {c+id} \)
iff \( a=c \) and \( b=d \)
(ii) \( \displaystyle{(a+ib)\pm (c+id) = (a+c)\pm i(b+d) } \)
(iii) \( \displaystyle{(a+ib) \cdot (c+id) = (ac-bd)+i(ad+bc)} \)
(iv) \( \displaystyle{ \frac{a+ib}{c+id} = {\frac{a+ib}{c+id} \cdot \frac{c-id}{c-id} = \frac{(ac+bd)+i(bc-ad)}{c^2+d^2} } } \)
where \( c-id \) is the conjugate of \( c+id \)


Examples



(1) Express the following in the form \( a+ib \)
(a) \( i^3(1+i)+i^5(3-i)+i^7(2+i) \)
(b) \( 2i(1-3i)+ 3(4-i) \)
(c) \( \displaystyle{(3+i)(4-2i)} \)
(d) \( \displaystyle{(6+2i)(1-3i)} \)
(e) \( \displaystyle{\frac{2-5i}{7+3i}} \)
(f) \( \displaystyle{\frac{1-5i}{3+2i}} \)


Solution

(a) \( i^3(1+i)+i^5(3-i)+i^7(2+i) \)
\( \displaystyle{ \implies {(i \cdot i^2)(1+i)+(i \cdot i^2 \cdot i^2)(3-i)+(i \cdot i^2 \cdot i^2 \cdot i^2)(2+i)}} \)
\( \displaystyle{ = -i(1+i)+i(3-i)-i(2+i)} \)
\( \displaystyle{-i+1+3i+1-2i+1 = 3} \)

(b) \( \displaystyle{2i(1-3i)+3(4-i)} \)
\( \displaystyle{= 2i-6 \cdot i^2+12-3i} \)
\( \displaystyle{= 2i+6+12-3i} \)
\( \displaystyle{= 18-i} \)

(c) \( \displaystyle{(3+i)(4-2i)} \)
\( \displaystyle{\implies 12-6i+4i-2 \cdot i^2} \)
\( \displaystyle{= 12-2i+2 = 14-2i} \)

(d) \( \displaystyle{(6+2i)(1-3i)} \)
\( \displaystyle{ \implies 6-18i+2i-6 \cdot i^2} \)
\( \displaystyle{= 6-16i+6 = 12-16i} \)

(e) \( \displaystyle{\frac{2-5i}{7+3i}} \)
\( \displaystyle{\implies \frac{2-5i}{7+3i} \cdot \frac{7-3i}{7-3i} } \)
\( \displaystyle{ = \frac{14-6i-35i+15 \cdot i^2}{49+9}} \)
\( \displaystyle{= \frac{14-41i-15}{58} = \frac{-(1+41i)}{58}} \)

(f) \( \displaystyle{\frac{1-5i}{3+2i} } \)
\( \displaystyle{\implies \frac{1-5i}{3+2i} \cdot \frac{3-2i}{3-2i} } \)
\( \displaystyle{ = \frac{3-2i-15i-10}{9+4}= \frac{-(7+17i)}{13}} \)


(2) Find the solutions of the equation
\( \displaystyle{ x^2+6x+18=0 } \)
in the form \( \displaystyle{a+ib } \). verify their correctness by substitution into the equation.

Solution

\( \displaystyle{ x^2+6x+18=0 } \)

Using the quadratic formula
\( \displaystyle{x= \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}} \)

We have
\( \displaystyle{x= \frac{-6 \pm \sqrt{6^2-4 \cdot 1 \cdot 18}}{2 \cdot 1}} \)

\( \displaystyle{= \frac{-6 \pm \sqrt{36-72}}{2} = \frac{-6 \pm \sqrt{-36}}{2}} \)

\( \displaystyle{= \frac{-6 \pm i \sqrt{36}}{2} = {\frac{-6 \pm 6i}{2}} = {-3 \pm 3i}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\therefore x={-3+3i}} \) or \( \displaystyle{x={-3-3i}} \)

To verify
When \( x={-3+3i} \)
\( \displaystyle{x^2+6x+18 \implies {(-3+3i)^2+6(-3+3i)+18}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies 9-18i-9-18+18i+18 = 0} \)

When \( \displaystyle{x={-3-3i}} \)
\( \displaystyle{x^2+6x+18 \implies {(-3-3i)^2+6(-3-3i)+18}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {9+18i-9-18-18i+18} = 0} \)
