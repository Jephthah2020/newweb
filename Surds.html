SURDS


Rules of Surds


(1) \( \displaystyle{ \sqrt{(a \times b)} = {\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b}}} \) and \( \displaystyle{\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{a} = a} \)

(2) \( \displaystyle{ \sqrt{a \over b} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}} \)

(3) \( \displaystyle{{a \sqrt{c}} \pm {b \sqrt{c}} = {(a \pm b) \sqrt{c}}} \)

(4) \( \displaystyle{{(a \sqrt{b})^2} = {(a)^2 (\sqrt{b})^2} = {a^2b}} \)

(5) \( \displaystyle{{a \sqrt{b}} \times {c \sqrt{d}} = {(a \times c) \sqrt{b \times d}}} \)

(6) \( \displaystyle{{a \sqrt{b}} = \sqrt{b \times a \times a}} \)

(7) \( \displaystyle{ \frac{a}{\sqrt{b}} = {{a \over {\sqrt{b}}} \times {\frac{\sqrt{b}}{\sqrt{b}}}} = \frac{a \sqrt{b}}{b}} \)

(8) \( \displaystyle{{\frac{a}{b+c \sqrt{d}}} = { {\frac{a}{b+c \sqrt{d}}} \times {\frac{b-c \sqrt{d}}{b-c \sqrt{d}}} } } \)

\( \displaystyle{ = \frac{ab-ac \sqrt{d}}{(b^2)-(c \sqrt(d))^2}} \)

(7) and (8) above are known as rationalization

To rationalize means to multiply both the numerator and the denominator with the conjugate of the Denominator.

For example, if the denominator is \( \displaystyle{3-2 \sqrt{5}} \)

then its conjugate is \( \displaystyle{3+2 \sqrt{5}} \)

Note: To get the conjugate, we only change, we only change the sign,

\( \displaystyle{+} \) to \( \displaystyle{-} \), or \( \displaystyle{-} \) to \( \displaystyle{+} \) .


Examples


(1) Simplify the following

(a) \( \displaystyle{\sqrt{50}} \)
(b) \( \displaystyle{\sqrt{125} - \sqrt{80}} \)
(c) \( \displaystyle{2 \sqrt{98}} \)
(d) \( \displaystyle{{\sqrt{6} \times {\sqrt{12}}}} \)


Solution

(a) \( \displaystyle{\sqrt{50}} \)

We need to express the surd in its simplest form, that

is, we make the number under the square root as small as possible.

For \( \displaystyle{\sqrt{50}} \), we need to find two numbers, one must be a perfect square
whereby,the product must be equal to the number under the square root.

for \( \displaystyle{\sqrt{50}} \), the two numbers that we can only use are

\( \displaystyle{25, 2} \), since \( \displaystyle{25} \) is a perfect square, and

\( \displaystyle{{25} \times 2 = 50} \)

\( \displaystyle{\therefore \sqrt{50} = \sqrt{{25} \times 2}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {\sqrt{25}} \times {\sqrt{2}}} \) (check Rule 1)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {5 \times {\sqrt{2}}} = {5 \sqrt{2}}} \)


(b) \( \displaystyle{\sqrt{125}- \sqrt{80}} \)

Again, we look for a perfect aquare and another

number such that its product gives \( \displaystyle{125} \).

we do the same thing to for \( \displaystyle{80} \).

\( \displaystyle{\sqrt{125} - \sqrt{80} = {\sqrt{{25} \times 5} - \sqrt{{16} \times 5}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {({\sqrt{25}} \times {\sqrt{5}})-({\sqrt{16}}\times {\sqrt{5}})}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {({5 \times {\sqrt{5}}})-({4 \times {\sqrt{5}}})} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {5 \sqrt{5} - 4 \sqrt{5}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies (5-4) \sqrt{5}} \) (check Rule 3)

\( \displaystyle{\implies \sqrt{5}} \)


(c) \( \displaystyle{2 \sqrt{98} = {2 \times {(\sqrt{98})}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {2 \times {(\sqrt{{49} \times 2})}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {{2 \times {({\sqrt{49}} \times {\sqrt{2}}})} } } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {{2 \times {(7 \times {\sqrt{2}})}}} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {2 \times {7 \sqrt{2}}} = {(2 \times 7) \sqrt{2}} = 14 \sqrt{2} } \)


(d) \( \displaystyle{{\sqrt{6}} \times {\sqrt{12}} = {\sqrt{6 \times {12}}} = \sqrt{72} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {\sqrt{{36} \times 2}} = {\sqrt{36}} \times {\sqrt{2}} } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {6 \times \sqrt{2}} = 6 \sqrt{2}} \)


(2) Rationalize

(a) \( \displaystyle{\frac{4}{\sqrt{6}}} \)

(b) \( \displaystyle{\frac{1+ \sqrt{5}}{3+ \sqrt{5}}} \)

Solution


(a) \( \displaystyle{\frac{4}{\sqrt{6}} = {{4 \over {\sqrt{6}}} \times {{\sqrt{6}} \over {\sqrt{6}}}} } \) (Check Rule 7)

\( \displaystyle{\implies { {\frac{4 \sqrt{6}}{6}} } = \frac{2 \sqrt{6}}{3} } \)


(b) \( \displaystyle{ {\frac{1+ \sqrt{5}}{3+ \sqrt{5}}} = { {\frac{1+ \sqrt{5}}{3+ \sqrt{5}}} \times {\frac{3-\sqrt{5}}{3-\sqrt{5}}} } } \) (check Rule 8)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {\frac{({1+ \sqrt{5}})({3- \sqrt{5}})}{(3)^2-(\sqrt{5})^2}} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {\frac{3- \sqrt{5} +5\sqrt{5} -5}{9-5}} = {\frac{(3-5)+(3-1) \sqrt{5}}{4}} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies \frac{2+2 \sqrt{5}}{4} = \frac{2(1+ \sqrt{5})}{4}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies \frac{1+ \sqrt{5}}{2}} \)


(3) Express \( \displaystyle{ \frac{(1+ \sqrt{5})}{2 + \frac{5}{\sqrt{5}}}} \) in the form \( \displaystyle{a+ \sqrt{b}} \), where \( \displaystyle{a} \) and \( \displaystyle{b} \) are integers.

Solution

\( \displaystyle{\frac{1+ \sqrt{5}}{2+ \frac{5}{\sqrt{5}}}} \)

First, we need to express the denominator in its simplest form.

now

\( \displaystyle{2+ \frac{5}{\sqrt{5}} = 2 + {({{\frac{5}{\sqrt{5}}} \times {\frac{\sqrt{5}}{\sqrt{5}}}})} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {2 + {\frac{5 \sqrt{5}}{5}} } = {2+ \sqrt{5}} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\therefore {2 + \frac{5}{\sqrt{5}}} ={2+ \sqrt{5}}} \)

So, \( \displaystyle{ {\frac{1+ \sqrt{5}}{2+ \frac{5}{\sqrt{5}}}} = {\frac{1+ \sqrt{5}}{2+ \sqrt{5}}} } \)
