INEQUALITIES



This is a branch of Mathematics which deals with the relationship between numbers that are not equal.

We say \( a \) is greater than \( b (a>b) \) if \( a-b \) is positive, and

We say \( a \) is less than \( b (a>b) \) if \( a-b \) is negative,.

That is

\( a > b \) if \( a-b > 0 \) and \( a < b \) if \( a-b < 0 \)

For example

\( 2 > -5 \) since \( 2-{(-5)} = 2+5 =7 > 0 \) (is positive).

\( -2 < 5 \) since \( -2 -5 = -7<0 \) (is negative).

However, there are two types of inequalities.

  1. Linear Inequalities
  2. Quadratic inequalities

We also have rules for inequalities

Rule 1: An equal quantity can be added(or subtracted) to

both sides of an inequality without changing the inequality.

Rule 2: An equal positive quantity may multiply(or divide)

both sides of an inequality without changing the inequality.

Rule 3: If both sides of an inequality are multiplied

or divided by a negative quantity, then the inequality is reversed.



Examples

(1) Solve the inequalities \( \displaystyle{3x+11 > 0} \) and \( \displaystyle{8-7x > 0} \).

(2) \( \displaystyle{2x^2-7x+9 < {x^2-2x+3}} \)

(3) Find \( x \) if \( \displaystyle{ |x+3| = 2} \)

(4) Find \( x \) if \( \displaystyle{|2x+3| < {1}} \)

(5) For what values of \( x \) is \( \displaystyle{\frac{2x-1}{x+3} < {2 \over 3}} \) ?


Solution


(1) \( \displaystyle{3x+11 > 0} \) and \( \displaystyle{8-7x > 0} \)

We solve \( \displaystyle{3x+11>0} \) first.

Subtracting \( 11 \) from both side of the inequality, we have

\( \displaystyle{3x+11-11>0-11} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies 3x>{-11}} \)

dividing both sides by \( 3 \), we have

\( \displaystyle{x = - \frac{11}{3}} \)

Now, we solve \( \displaystyle{8-7x>0} \)

Subtracting \( 8 \) from both sides, we have

\( \displaystyle{-7x > -8} \)

dividing both sides by \( -7 \) , (the inequality is reversed)

\( \displaystyle{x < \frac{8}{7}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\therefore x>{ - \frac{11}{3}}} \) and \( \displaystyle{x <{8 \over 7}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {- \frac{11}{3} < x < {8 \over 7}}} \)


(2) \( \displaystyle{2x^2-7x+9 < {x^2-2x+3}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {x^2-5x+6 < 0}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {(x-2)(x-3) < 0}} \cdots \) (i)

Now, the equation is equal to zero if \( \displaystyle{x=2} \) or \( \displaystyle{x=3} \)

\( \displaystyle{(x-3) } \) becomes \( \displaystyle{1-3 = -2} \) (which is negative)

But \( \displaystyle{x>3} \) does not satisfy the inequality.

Only \( \displaystyle{{2 < {x} <{3}}} \) satisfies the inequality.

Hence, \( \displaystyle{2 < {x} <{3}} \)
